Full description

The campaign funds a mobile STEM laboratory intended for underserved schools with more limited access to technological equipment. The project includes the purchase of robotics kits, educational notebooks, and teaching materials for mobile sessions led by tutors. The goal is to increase accessibility to practical experiences in science, coding, and making, with particular attention to vulnerable school environments.

Campaign updates

Laboratory van setup has started April 18, 2026
The organization confirmed the purchase order for the internal workstations, technical cabinets, and safety equipment needed to prepare the mobile laboratory van.
First educational kits delivered April 4, 2026
The first robotics kits and entry-level 3D printers were delivered to EduSpark Association and will be used during the pilot sessions with partner schools.
Partner schools selected March 22, 2026
Five schools in the Pisa area were selected for the initial program. The organization is coordinating tutor schedules and classroom availability.
